Chief Executive Officer

Jonathan R. Hargreaves

CEO & Founder · Gravitex Paint Manufacturing

Jonathan Robert Hargreaves has spent over 25 years at the intersection of materials science and business strategy. A graduate of Georgia Tech with a BSc in Chemical Engineering and an MBA from Emory University's Goizueta Business School, Jonathan entered the coatings industry in 1998 as a formulation chemist for a regional paint distributor.

By 2001, he had identified a persistent gap in the market: mid-tier manufacturers were producing paints with inconsistent quality controls, using outdated resin formulations, and offering little to no scientific backing for their product claims. He believed — correctly, as it turned out — that a manufacturer committed to rigorous chemistry, transparency, and genuine client service could command a loyal market.

In March 2002, Jonathan founded Gravitex Paint with a $340,000 seed investment, a team of three chemists, and a 4,000 sq. ft. pilot production facility in East Atlanta. Within two years, the company had secured its first commercial construction contract and begun distribution across the southeastern United States.

"We don't just produce paint. We engineer confidence — the confidence that every wall, every structure, every surface we touch will stand the test of time with elegance and integrity. Quality is not a department at Gravitex. It is a culture."

Today, under Jonathan's continued leadership, Gravitex operates from a 120,000 sq. ft. manufacturing campus, employs over 340 professionals across operations, R&D, sales, and design, and exports product to 14 countries. He serves on the board of the American Coatings Association and chairs the sustainability committee for the National Paint and Coatings Association.

Outside the boardroom, Jonathan is an avid fly fisherman, a mentor in the Atlanta Entrepreneurs Network, and a passionate advocate for STEM education in underserved communities — a cause Gravitex supports through its annual Foundation Grant Program.

Company History

Key Milestones Under Jonathan's Leadership

From a startup lab to a nationally recognized manufacturer — the Gravitex story, year by year.

2002

Gravitex Paint Founded

Jonathan Hargreaves incorporated Gravitex Paint in Atlanta, GA, with a $340K seed investment, three chemists, and a mandate to manufacture premium-grade coatings backed by real science.

2005

First Major Commercial Contract

Gravitex secured a $2.1M contract to supply exterior coatings for the Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport expansion project — putting the brand on the national map.

2008

ISO 9001 Certification Achieved

Gravitex became the first paint manufacturer in Georgia to achieve ISO 9001:2008 certification, establishing a quality management benchmark that the company continues to maintain and exceed.

2012

Armour Industrial Series Launched

The company expanded into industrial coatings with the launch of the Armour epoxy and polyurethane series, opening significant new revenue streams in manufacturing, logistics, and marine infrastructure.

2016

New 120,000 Sq. Ft. Campus Opened

Jonathan led a $28M capital investment to construct Gravitex's state-of-the-art production campus in East Atlanta, tripling manufacturing capacity and creating 180 new jobs.

2019

International Distribution Launched

Gravitex began exporting to its first international markets: Canada, the UK, and Nigeria — beginning the company's transformation from a national to a global brand.

2022

Zero-VOC Product Line Introduced

Marking the company's 20th anniversary, Jonathan announced the complete reformulation of Gravitex's residential line to include zero-VOC options across all product categories.

2024

NACE Industry Leader of the Year

Jonathan was named Industry Leader of the Year by the National Association of Corrosion Engineers, recognizing Gravitex's contributions to anti-corrosion coating innovation.

2026

Distribution in 14 Countries & Counting

With the recent addition of markets in Southeast Asia and South America, Gravitex Paint now distributes to 14 countries, with a target of 20 by 2028 as part of Jonathan's Global Vision Plan.
